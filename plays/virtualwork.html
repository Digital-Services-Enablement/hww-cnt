Working on a Dispersed Team

Remote work opportunities are expanding exponentially and virtual teams are finding ways to be just as effective as those physically grounded ones. Use this play to overcome virtual team challenges, improve team cohesion, and optimize your team’s performance.

Use this play to...

Use this play to build a strong and collaborative virtual culture, explore cloud based communications and collaborative tools, and determine do’s and don’ts to make your team experience a success.

  • Establish a remote team
  • Lead or manage a virtual team
  • Be a successful remote worker

Running the play

This play was created with the virtual* team manager as the quarterback however it is also very relevant for remote team employees. As a virtual team manager establishing and maintaining a displaced team does not have to be any more challenging than a team working at a physical location. The key to success starts with the fundamentals – heavy on the communication with a side of effective tools and a sprinkle of positive work culture. Follow these 4 checklist style steps to give your virtual team an identity and all the tools necessary to succeed: Establish your team’s culture, ensure the environment is appropriate, fill the team’s tool box, and consult the virtual team Do’s and Don’ts document.

* For the purpose of this play virtual, remote, and dispersed are all used interchangeably.

Build Culture

Your success will largely be dependent on your team’s foundation. Focus on promoting a positive team culture and consider the following:

  1. How comfortable is each member working and collaborating remotely.
  2. Communicate!!! As much as possible and preferably by video. Face to Face time is invaluable to your team member’s feelings of inclusion.
  3. Establish expectations and define roles! Your team members can’t read minds but knowing what is expected of them and their roles will dramatically improve team effectiveness.
  4. Set regular meeting times and have a shared task calendar to keep everyone on track.
  5. Delegate and avoid micro-managing
  6. Budget permitting try to host in-person meetings throughout the year to strengthen team bonding.

Tip

Personalize things and keep things lighthearted. Encourage your team to share their personal lives or have side chats that allow employees to bond and connect as if they were together in the office. Consider having a random question of the day/week thread or make it a point to start meetings with a review of everyone’s weekend by asking ‘how are things going outside of work’? How about trying a ‘work remotely at any location of your choosing other than your regular location’ day to help team bonding. Personalizing things for a dispersed team can improve your team’s feelings of belonging.

Optimize your environment

Where exactly are your employees working? It is important to both assess the environments your team is working in and to provide optimized spaces to ensure maximum collaboration and performance. Here is a checklist of questions to ask yourself:

  1. Do your employees have the appropriate set-ups for virtual work? Consider location of work and what does each employee have access to or what are they missing (ie: video conferencing rooms, tablets, webcam, phones, access to required networks and software, printers… etc)?
  2. Time Zones? Can you mitigate the effects of multiple time zones on your group's day to day work?
  3. Have you discussed your employees work hours to ensure that any differences do not negatively impact your team’s ability to function and collaborate. Be sure to compromise where necessary.

    4. Tip

    Develop an onboarding checklist to help ensure that you have provide the basics for each employee to thrive as a dispersed worker. Take a look at the Annex A - TC Virtual Onboarding Checklist  for some initial ideas or you can also do an internet search for more onboarding checklist ideas.

Toolbox Assessment

Does your team have the tools necessary to succeed? If possible, determine by consensus which programs are best suited for your team to be effective. Today’s collaboration software options are limitless, and most will allow your team to communicate (voice, written, and video), collaborate, share and work on the same files, track/comment/revise work, and provide task calendars amongst other features. Consider using the following to help your virtual team reach its full potential*:

  • MS Teams
  • Slack
  • Trello
  • Skype
  • Asana
  • Google Drive
  • MS Onedrive
  • Zoom

Do consider the security level of content as well as your values and ethics responsibilities when using any collaborative tool.
